Prolific Study

Daily Dilemmas
Label Verification Task

In this study, you will review a series of everyday moral dilemmas. For each dilemma, two possible actions are presented — a Do action and a Don't Do action — each labelled with one or more moral foundations. Your task is to evaluate whether the assigned moral foundation labels accurately capture the ethical dimensions of each action.

The study consists of 22 dilemmas and should take approximately 15–20 minutes. Please read each dilemma carefully before responding.

Step 2 of 3 — Instructions

How to annotate

Each dilemma presents two actions. For every moral foundation label assigned to an action, you will indicate your agreement using a 3-point scale:

  • Disagree — The label does not fit this action.
  • Neutral — The label partially fits, or you are unsure.
  • Agree — The label accurately captures this action.

If you select Disagree or Neutral, a dropdown will appear asking you to choose which foundation you think better applies. The dashed line separates the shared foundations from the differentiating foundation — the one that distinguishes the two actions. Hover over any foundation name to see its definition.

The six moral foundations

Care Intuitions about avoiding emotional and physical damage to another individual. Example: helping an injured stranger.
Equality Intuitions about equal treatment and equal outcome for individuals. Example: treating all patients with the same standard of care.
Proportionality Intuitions about individuals getting rewarded in proportion to their merit or contribution. Example: paying workers according to their effort, not seniority.
Loyalty Intuitions about cooperating with ingroups and competing with outgroups. Example: keeping a secret to protect a friend.
Authority Intuitions about deference toward legitimate authorities and the defense of traditions, seen as providing stability and order. Example: following institutional rules even when inconvenient.
Purity Intuitions about avoiding bodily and spiritual contamination and degradation. Example: refusing to desecrate a sacred object.
